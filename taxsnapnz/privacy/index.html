Tax Snap (NZ) — Privacy Policy

Last updated: June 14, 2026

Tax Snap (NZ) is an offline reference tool for New Zealand tax statutes, IRD rulings, IRD guidance, and IRD forms. It is published by an independent developer and is not affiliated with Inland Revenue, the Treasury, or the New Zealand Government.

This page explains, in plain language, exactly what data the app handles — and what it doesn't.

1. What the app collects

Nothing. Tax Snap (NZ) does not collect, transmit, store on a server, or share any personal information.

There are no user accounts. There is no registration. There is no login. There is no email or phone number. The app does not ask for, and does not have access to, your IRD number, your taxable income, your client list, your bank details, or any other personal information.

2. What is on your device

The following are stored locally on your iPhone or iPad only and never leave it:

  • Favourites — sections, rulings, guidance, forms, and rates you star.
  • Collections — folders you create with optional client labels (a label you typed yourself; the app cannot validate or use it for any purpose).
  • Notes — any free-text note you attach to a favourited item.
  • Recently viewed — the last few items you opened, to make navigation faster.
  • Settings — theme, app-icon choice, citation style, font-size preference.
  • Onboarding state — a flag remembering you've seen the welcome screen.
  • Premium unlock state — whether you've purchased the Supporter In-App Purchase, plus a developer debug-unlock flag for testing.

All of this is held in standard iOS sandboxed storage (UserDefaults and the local SQLite database that ships with the app). Apple's iCloud Backup will include this data only if you have iCloud Backup enabled in iOS Settings, in which case it is encrypted under your iCloud credentials. The app itself never reads, writes to, or syncs anything to iCloud.

If you uninstall Tax Snap (NZ), all of the above is removed by iOS along with the app.

3. What is bundled with the app

The app ships with a read-only SQLite database (~60 MB) containing:

  • Sections of the Income Tax Act 2007, Tax Administration Act 1994, Goods and Services Tax Act 1985, and KiwiSaver Act 2006 — sourced from legislation.govt.nz under Crown copyright (Legislation Act 2019).
  • IRD public rulings, product rulings, interpretation statements, standard practice statements, questions-we've-been-asked, technical decision summaries, and operational statements — sourced from taxtechnical.ird.govt.nz under Crown copyright (CC BY 4.0).
  • Tax Information Bulletins (TIBs) — sourced from the same site.
  • IRD form descriptions (IR3, IR4, IR6, GST101A, KS1–KS10, etc.).
  • Tax rates and thresholds for the current and recent income years.
This data is public-domain Crown copyright material. It is not, and never has been, your personal information.

4. Network activity

Tax Snap (NZ) makes no network requests of any kind under normal operation. Every search, every detail view, and every cross-link chip is served from the local SQLite database that ships with the app.

The only network calls Apple's StoreKit framework may initiate on the app's behalf are:

  • Verifying your one-time Supporter In-App Purchase, when you tap "Buy me a coffee" or "Restore Purchase". This is handled entirely by Apple's StoreKit. Apple's Privacy Policy governs how StoreKit handles your purchase information.
  • Opening an external link in Safari when you tap the "Open in IRD/legislation.govt.nz" button on a detail page. This loads the source page on the public IRD or NZ Legislation website using your iOS web browser. The developer of this app never sees that you did so.

5. Analytics, crash reporting, advertising, tracking

There are none.

  • No third-party analytics SDK.
  • No crash reporting SDK.
  • No advertising SDK.
  • No tracking SDK.
  • No fingerprinting.
  • No App Tracking Transparency (ATT) prompt because there is nothing to track.

If you experience a crash, Apple's standard iOS crash reporter (the same one all iOS apps use) may include a stack trace in the system-wide diagnostics that you can choose to share with developers via iOS Settings → Privacy & Security → Analytics & Improvements. The developer of Tax Snap (NZ) receives only the anonymous, aggregated reports Apple chooses to provide via App Store Connect.

6. Children

Tax Snap (NZ) is intended for tax practitioners, accountants, and self-lodging adult taxpayers in New Zealand. It is not directed at children and does not knowingly collect any information from anyone.

7. Your rights

Because the app does not collect or hold any personal information about you, there is no personal information to access, correct, port, or delete. If you wish to delete the local-on-device data (favourites, notes, collections, etc.), the standard iOS uninstall flow is the canonical mechanism — long-press the app icon → Remove App → Delete App.

If you would like to delete only the local-on-device data while keeping the app installed, the relevant levers are in Settings:

  • Settings → Reset → Clear Recently Viewed removes the recently-viewed list.
  • Settings → Reset → Reset Onboarding clears the onboarding-seen flag.
  • Favourites, Notes, and Collections can be deleted individually inside their respective tabs.

Under the New Zealand Privacy Act 2020 you have the right to ask whether an organisation holds personal information about you and to request access or correction. Because Tax Snap (NZ)'s developer holds no personal information about you, no such request is necessary.

8. Source-of-truth disclaimer

Tax Snap (NZ) is a reference tool. It does not constitute tax advice. New Zealand tax law is complex and changes regularly. For specific tax matters, consult a registered Tax Agent or a CA ANZ–qualified accountant. Always check the canonical source — legislation.govt.nz for statutory text and ird.govt.nz for IRD interpretations.
