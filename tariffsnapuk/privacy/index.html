Privacy Policy — Tariff Snap (UK)

Last updated 2 June 2026 · For Tariff Snap (UK) v1.0+

Short version

Tariff Snap (UK) does not collect any user data of any kind. No analytics, no tracking, no third-party SDKs that observe your behaviour. Everything you create in the app — favourites, collections, notes, recent searches — stays on your device.

What data we collect

None. Specifically:

  • No personal information (name, email, phone, address, IP address, device identifiers).
  • No usage analytics or telemetry.
  • No crash reports sent to any server.
  • No advertising identifiers (IDFA, IDFV).
  • No location data.
  • No contacts, photos, microphone, camera, or other sensor access.

What stays on your device

The following is stored locally and never leaves your iPhone or iPad:

  • Favourite commodity codes (SwiftData)
  • Collections and the codes you add to them (SwiftData)
  • Per-code notes (SwiftData)
  • Recent searches (UserDefaults)
  • Theme, font size, NI Mode, and onboarding preferences (UserDefaults)

All of this is deleted when you delete the app from your device.

Network activity

The only network requests Tariff Snap (UK) initiates are to Apple's StoreKit servers when you tap the optional "Support Developer" purchase, and when you tap "Restore Purchases". Those requests are handled by Apple's standard StoreKit framework and are subject to Apple's Privacy Policy, not ours.

The bundled UK Tariff database is read from the app binary itself. No fetch happens at runtime.

Third-party SDKs

Tariff Snap (UK) ships with one third-party software dependency: GRDB.swift, a SQLite toolkit used to read the bundled UK Tariff database. GRDB.swift runs entirely on-device and does not transmit any information.

Children's privacy

Tariff Snap (UK) is not directed at children under 13. It is rated 4+ for safe content but is designed for professional use by customs brokers, importers, exporters, and freight forwarders.

Changes to this policy

If we ever change this policy, the new version will be published at this same URL with an updated "Last updated" date at the top, and the change will be summarised in the app's release notes ("What's New in This Version") for that version.
