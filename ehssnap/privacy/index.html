EHS Snap — Privacy Policy

Last updated 2026-06-03 · v1.0

EHS Snap is built around a simple promise: everything you do in the app stays on your device. No accounts, no analytics, no cloud sync, no behavioral tracking.

Data we do not collect

  • No personal information (name, email, phone, location)
  • No account or sign-in — there is no server to sign in to
  • No usage analytics, crash reporting, or telemetry of any kind
  • No advertising identifier, no third-party ad SDKs
  • No usage of contacts, calendars, photos, microphone, or camera

What lives on your device

The regulation text (29 CFR Part 1910, 30 CFR Parts 46–90), OSHA Letters of Interpretation, and the NIOSH Pocket Guide chemical limits are bundled offline in a read-only SQLite file inside the app. None of that content is sent anywhere when you search it.

Your favorites, collections, notes, and recent items are saved locally by SwiftData under EHS Snap's app sandbox. iOS's standard encrypted-at-rest protection applies. They are not transmitted off the device, and they go away when you delete the app.

Network access

EHS Snap makes one kind of network request only: Apple's StoreKit, when you tap the optional Supporter upgrade in Settings. That request is handled entirely by iOS and Apple's servers under Apple's App Store privacy policy. EHS Snap itself never opens a network connection.

Tapping "View on osha.gov" inside a Letter of Interpretation hands the URL to Safari (or your default browser) — the request is made by the browser, not by EHS Snap. Anything that happens after that is governed by osha.gov and your browser's privacy policies.

App Tracking Transparency

EHS Snap does not request the App Tracking Transparency permission and does not pass any identifier to advertisers, brokers, or any third party. There is no tracking to opt into.

Children's privacy

EHS Snap is a professional reference tool intended for adult workplace safety professionals. It does not collect data of any kind from any user, child or adult.

Data sources (not affiliated)

The bundled dataset is built from public-domain federal government works: eCFR.gov (Office of the Federal Register), osha.gov (OSHA Letters of Interpretation), and the CDC NIOSH Pocket Guide to Chemical Hazards. Changes to this policy

Material changes will be reflected here with a new "Last updated" date. Because EHS Snap doesn't have your contact information, the canonical way to see updates is to revisit this page.

Contact
